What you're feeling is real.

You don't have to explain it. You don't have to justify it to anyone, including yourself. Whatever brought you here tonight — or this morning, or this afternoon — the pain you're carrying is real, and it matters. The fact that you're reading this means something in you is still reaching for light, even if you can't feel it. That took courage.

You're not the first.

Thousands of years ago, someone felt exactly what you're feeling right now. They didn't hide it. They wrote it down. These aren't lessons. They're the words of people who sat in the same darkness and spoke into it.

Others have been here.

After my mom died, I stopped feeling anything. Not sad — empty. Like the world kept moving and I was watching from behind glass. I didn't want to die, but I didn't want to keep going either. One night I opened a Bible she'd left me and found a verse she'd underlined — Psalm 34:18. I don't know why, but it broke something open. The grief didn't leave, but I wasn't alone in it anymore.

Everyone thought I was fine. Good job, good family, always smiling. But every night I'd sit in my car in the driveway, not wanting to go inside, not wanting to go anywhere. Just tired of being me. I finally told God the truth — not a prayer, just the truth: "I can't do this." That was the first honest thing I'd said in years. And somehow, that tiny crack of honesty was enough for light to get in.

I was convinced I was too far gone. The things I'd done, the patterns I couldn't break — I figured God had a limit, and I'd passed it. Then someone told me something I didn't believe at first: "Grace doesn't wait for you to be fixed. It meets you in the middle of the mess." I'm still in the middle. But I'm not alone in it, and I'm not the same person I was. That's enough for today.

When you're ready.

No rush. These will be here whenever you need them.

  • Talk to someone

    Call 988 anytime, day or night. Or text HOME to 741741. Real people. No judgment.

  • Find a counselor

    You deserve professional support. Psychology Today's therapist finder lets you search by location, insurance, and specialty.

  • Reach out to a church

    Many churches offer free counseling. You don't have to be a member. You don't have to believe anything specific. You just have to show up.

  • Call a friend

    Even if it's been a while. Even if you think they won't understand. Make the call. You might be surprised.
